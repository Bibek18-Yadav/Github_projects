CLASSIFICATION OF ELEMENTS AND PERIODIC TABLE – Grade 11 Chemistry (Nepal Curriculum)
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------

1. INTRODUCTION
Classification of elements arranges elements based on their properties, making it easier to study and predict their behavior. The Periodic Table is a systematic arrangement of elements in order of increasing atomic number, highlighting periodicity in properties.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------
2. EARLY ATTEMPTS OF CLASSIFICATION
- **Dobereiner’s Triads:** Grouped three elements with similar properties; middle element's atomic mass is approx. average of other two.
- **Newlands’ Law of Octaves:** Elements arranged by increasing atomic mass; properties repeated every eighth element.
- **Mendeleev’s Periodic Table:** Arranged by increasing atomic mass; left gaps for undiscovered elements; predicted properties of missing elements.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------
3. MODERN PERIODIC TABLE
- **Based on Atomic Number:** Modern Periodic Table arranges elements by increasing atomic number (Z).
- **Periods and Groups:** 
  - **Period:** Horizontal row, properties gradually change.
  - **Group:** Vertical column, elements have similar chemical properties.
- **Blocks:** s-block, p-block, d-block, f-block based on valence electron configuration.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------
4. PERIODIC PROPERTIES
- **Atomic Radius:** Distance from nucleus to outermost electron; decreases across a period, increases down a group.
- **Ionization Energy:** Energy required to remove an electron; increases across a period, decreases down a group.
- **Electron Affinity:** Energy change on gaining an electron; generally more negative across a period.
- **Electronegativity:** Ability to attract electrons in a bond; increases across a period, decreases down a group.
- **Metallic and Non-Metallic Character:** Metals → left, non-metals → right; metallic character decreases across a period, increases down a group.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------
5. CLASSIFICATION OF ELEMENTS
- **Metals:** Malleable, ductile, good conductors, low ionization energy.
- **Non-Metals:** Brittle, poor conductors, high ionization energy.
- **Metalloids (Semi-Metals):** Properties of both metals and non-metals.
- **Representative Elements:** s- and p-block elements.
- **Transition Elements:** d-block, variable oxidation states, form colored compounds.
- **Inner Transition Elements:** f-block, lanthanides, actinides.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------
6. IMPORTANCE OF PERIODIC TABLE
- Predicts physical and chemical properties of elements.
- Helps in studying reactivity trends and valence.
- Provides systematic arrangement aiding chemical research and industrial applications.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------
SUMMARY
The classification of elements and the Periodic Table simplify understanding of elements’ properties and chemical behavior. Modern periodic law (based on atomic number) provides a universal framework for organizing all known elements.
