--- Trigonometry — Grade 11 (Nepal) ---

1. Properties of a Triangle
   • Sine Law: a/sin A = b/sin B = c/sin C
   • Cosine Law: c² = a² + b² – 2ab cos C (and its variants)
   • Tangent Law: (a – b)/(a + b) = tan((A – B)/2) / tan((A + B)/2)
   • Projection Formula: b = c cos A + a cos C
   • Half-Angle Formulas: sin(A/2) = √[(s – b)(s – c)/(bc)], etc.

2. Solution of a Triangle
   • Given SAS: Use Cosine Law to find the third side, then Sine Law for remaining angles.
   • Given ASA or AAS: Use Sine Law to find unknown sides.
   • Given SSA: Ambiguous case — may have 0, 1, or 2 possible triangles.
   • Area of a Triangle: (1/2) ab sin C, or √[s(s – a)(s – b)(s – c)].

3. Inverse Trigonometric Functions and Trigonometric Equations
   • Definition: y = arcsin(x), y = arccos(x), y = arctan(x)
   • Domains and Ranges: 
       sin⁻¹(x) → x ∈ [–1, 1], y ∈ [–π/2, π/2]
       cos⁻¹(x) → x ∈ [–1, 1], y ∈ [0, π]
       tan⁻¹(x) → x ∈ ℝ, y ∈ (–π/2, π/2)
   • General Solutions:
       sin θ = k → θ = nπ + (–1)ⁿ arcsin(k)
       cos θ = k → θ = ±arccos(k) + 2nπ
       tan θ = k → θ = arctan(k) + nπ
   • Double and Multiple Angle Identities: sin 2A, cos 2A, tan 2A, etc.

4. Key Trigonometric Identities
   • sin²θ + cos²θ = 1
   • 1 + tan²θ = sec²θ ; 1 + cot²θ = cosec²θ
   • sin(A ± B) = sin A cos B ± cos A sin B
   • cos(A ± B) = cos A cos B ∓ sin A sin B
   • sin 2A = 2 sin A cos A
   • cos 2A = cos²A – sin²A = 1 – 2 sin²A = 2 cos²A – 1

5. Quadrants and Signs of Trigonometric Functions
   • I Quadrant (0°–90°): All positive
   • II Quadrant (90°–180°): Only sine positive
   • III Quadrant (180°–270°): Only tangent positive
   • IV Quadrant (270°–360°): Only cosine positive
   • Even/Odd Properties: sin(–θ) = –sin θ, cos(–θ) = cos θ, tan(–θ) = –tan θ

6. Applications of Trigonometry
   • Heights and Distances
   • Use in Geometry (e.g., finding sides and angles in polygons)
   • Proving Trigonometric Identities
   • Graphs of sin, cos, tan functions (optional topic)
